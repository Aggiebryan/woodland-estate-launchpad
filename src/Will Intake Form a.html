We treat all client information with the utmost confidentiality. Our firm takes every precaution to safeguard your privacy, and the data you submit is protected using industry-standard 256-bit SSL encryption.

GUARDIAN DESIGNATIONS FOR MINOR CHILDREN

If you have minor children, you need to designate guardians who will be responsible for your children's care if something happens to you. There are two types of guardians: Guardian of the Person (physical care) and Guardian of the Estate (financial affairs).

TEMPORARY GUARDIAN DESIGNATIONS FOR MINOR CHILDREN

If you are unavailable and your child’s permanent guardian cannot reach your child right away, Texas law allows you to name a temporary guardian who can step in at once. Use this section to identify the person you trust to act as a first responder. Until the permanent guardian arrives, that temporary guardian may take custody of your child, make all necessary decisions—including medical decisions—and keep your child out of protective custody.
